About Me

Learn about my research journey and scientific interests in supramolecular chemistry

Kazi Md Zubaer

I'm a researcher specializing in organic supramolecular chemistry, with a focus on designing and synthesizing novel molecular systems that self-assemble into complex architectures with tailored functions.

My work bridges the gap between molecular design and material properties, exploring how non-covalent interactions can be harnessed to create functional materials with applications in sensing, catalysis, and nanotechnology.

I'm currently pursuing my research at the University of South Carolina, where we investigate the fundamental principles governing molecular self-assembly and develop new supramolecular systems with advanced properties.

Research Areas

Key focus areas of my supramolecular chemistry research

Molecular Self-Assembly

Design and synthesis of organic molecules that spontaneously organize into well-defined nanostructures through non-covalent interactions.

Host-Guest Chemistry

Development of molecular receptors with tailored cavities for selective recognition and binding of specific guest molecules.

Supramolecular Materials

Fabrication of functional materials with stimuli-responsive, self-healing, or adaptive properties based on supramolecular principles.

Characterization Techniques

Advanced analytical methods including NMR, X-ray crystallography, and microscopy to study supramolecular structures and dynamics.

Selected Publications

A selection of my recent research publications in high-impact journals

2023

Dynamic Covalent Assembly of Stimuli-Responsive Supramolecular Cages

Nature Chemistry June 2023 DOI: 10.1038/s41557-023-01234-6

We report a new class of stimuli-responsive supramolecular cages that can reversibly assemble and disassemble in response to external triggers, enabling controlled encapsulation and release of guest molecules.

2022

Chiral Self-Sorting in Supramolecular Polymers

Journal of the American Chemical Society August 2022 DOI: 10.1021/jacs.2c04567

This study explores the mechanisms of chiral self-sorting in supramolecular polymers, revealing how molecular chirality influences the formation of one-dimensional nanostructures with controlled helicity.

2021

Photoresponsive Host-Guest Systems Based on Azobenzene-Modified Macrocycles

Angewandte Chemie International Edition March 2021 DOI: 10.1002/anie.202107654

We developed a series of azobenzene-modified macrocycles whose binding affinity can be optically switched, enabling light-controlled molecular recognition and release processes.
